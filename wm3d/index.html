World Model · Robotics · Native 3D

World models should predict geometry, not just pixels.

WM3D is a native-3D, action-conditioned world model for robots. Instead of treating the future as a 2D video latent, it keeps the world state as depth, camera pose, 3D points, robot state, task text, and future action chunks — then renders video as a downstream view of that state.

Problem

Robots do not act on pixels. They change the 3D world.

For embodied manipulation, a future video is only the surface. The causal object is the scene geometry: where the gripper is, where objects are, how depth changes, and which action moved them.

01

Pixels are ambiguous

A single RGB frame can correspond to many different depths, camera poses, and contact states. Pixel prediction can look plausible while hiding the state needed for action.

02

Video latents hide causality

Pure video-world models can learn texture dynamics, but the action→geometry relation is indirect. The model is rewarded for matching appearance, not for representing what moved in 3D.

03

Planning needs inspectable state

To propose, imagine, rank, and execute actions, the model must expose depth, pose, progress, and feasibility — not only a pretty clip.

Research question: can a robot world model keep explicit native-3D state as the core, while still producing action-aware, high-quality RGB rollouts?
Positioning

Where WM3D sits among world models

The point is not “another video generator.” The point is a different state-space choice for robot prediction.

Video-latent first

Pixels / video as state

Representative systems emphasize future frames or latent video tokens.

  • Strong visual prior and photorealism.
  • Action grounding often enters through conditioning.
  • Geometry and contact are implicit inside the video representation.
WM3D

Native 3D as state, video as read-out

WM3D keeps the world state explicit: depth, point/geometry tokens, camera/robot pose, task text, and future action chunks.

  • Action-conditioned dynamics are trained in 3D.
  • RGB rendering is auxiliary, not the owner of the state.
  • Progress/proposer/policy heads can attach to the same state.
Control-state first

Compact dynamics / policy models

Low-dimensional dynamics are efficient and useful for control, but often lose the rich visuospatial structure of manipulation scenes.

  • Good for stable policy learning.
  • Harder to reason about open-world objects and views.
  • Less direct bridge to visual imagination.
Design lesson from modern video-action models: action must interact with the generative video path, not be a late weak adapter. WM3D's difference: the video path is added beside a native-3D core, instead of replacing it.
Architecture

A native-3D core with an action-aware RGB branch

The new structure makes the ownership boundary explicit: WM3D owns world state and action reasoning; the video backbone learns to render/action-condition the rollout without taking over the policy.

Before

Small RGB decoder was not enough

The early path validated motion and dynamics, but a small decoder hit a fidelity ceiling and could not be the final visual interface.

Now

Strong video prior, trained as action-video modeling

The RGB branch uses a stronger video backbone and lets action/video tokens interact inside the generator, instead of only conditioning from the outside.

Guardrail

RGB supports the world model; it does not replace it

Depth, point/geometry, pose, action, proposer, progress, and policy objectives stay in the WM3D core. Video is a rendered view of the imagined state.

Outcome

One loop: propose → imagine → rank → execute

The same world state can feed prediction metrics, counterfactual action tests, video rollouts, progress scoring, and downstream LIBERO-style action policies.

Scale

Built on broad robot video, cached as geometry

The pipeline converts robot videos into reusable native-3D supervision, so the model can train on large action-labeled windows without running the 3D foundation model online.

Status

What is solved, what is active, what comes next

✓ Native-3D dynamics

Open-loop prediction and counterfactual action metrics support the central claim: actions change the learned 3D state, not only the rendered pixels.

◐ Action-aware RGB branch

The new architecture replaces weak passive rendering with a video-action path while enforcing that the WM3D core remains the owner.

◐ Closed-loop planning

Best current LIBERO spatial policy score is 76.8%; the next goal is to make world-model imagination improve policy selection reliably, not just offline metrics.

WM3D reframes robot world modeling as a state-space problem: predict the future in native 3D, use actions as interventions on geometry, and render video as a view of the imagined world.
