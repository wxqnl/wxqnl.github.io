WM3D — modeling the future in
native 3D, not in 2D video.

Most world models predict the future in pixel or video-latent space. WM3D moves the world state onto explicit 3D geometry — world points + camera pose + depth, read out by a frozen feed-forward 3D foundation model (VGGT) — and predicts how a given action chunk pushes that geometry forward in time. For embodied manipulation, modeling the world in 3D is the more physically grounded, more transferable route.

Positioning

Why native 3D?

A single video frame is consistent with many different 3D structures and camera motions. In 2D, the coupling between action and geometry stays implicit — the model is good at "looks like", not at "is physically right, and can I act on it." WM3D makes the 3D structure the thing being predicted.

2D / VIDEO LATENT The mainstream

Dreamer, GAIA, Genie, DIAMOND/IRIS, Sora-as-world-model. State = video latent or RGB frame; at most depth-augmented 2.5D. Action→geometry causality is a byproduct of texture prediction.

NATIVE 3D WM3D

State = explicit 3D — world points (pointmap) + camera pose + depth. The model predicts where the action moves the scene's 3D structure, making action→geometry an explicit training target, not a side effect of pixels.

Honest boundary. 3D-aware generation is a heating-up area — we make no "world-first" claim. What is uncommon and defensible is using VGGT-style feed-forward 3D (pointmap + pose) as the world model's state and supervision target, and wiring it as the world engine of a VLA closed loop.
Method

One dual-stream trunk, many pluggable heads

A frozen VGGT turns observations into native-3D tokens. A dual-stream trunk predicts the future 3D tokens conditioned on an action chunk. Pluggable heads land that prediction as geometry, pixels, scores, proposals, or video.
